A Point of View

Perspectives on modern data infrastructure, semantic engineering, and enterprise AI orchestration

About This Work

DataHanle explores how enterprises can build durable AI systems by treating data architecture and semantic foundations as first-class concerns. The core belief: governance, context, and intentionality must precede AI capability deployment.

Unifying Themes

1. Business Processes as Primary AI Artifacts

Enterprise AI should begin with how business actually works—not with tables, APIs, or siloed models. When AI orchestrates across business processes, it needs shared understanding of intent, governance, and systems.

See: The Enterprise AI Hypothesis

2. Conformed Dimensions as Semantic Foundation

Without strict alignment on shared vocabulary, AI agents hallucinate and produce inconsistent results. Conformed dimensions—rooted in classical data warehousing—become essential guardrails for LLM-driven automation.

See: Conformed Dimensions in an AI-first Enterprise

3. Governance That Scales with Autonomy

As enterprises automate more processes with AI, the cost of a semantic inconsistency grows exponentially. Governance is not a constraint—it is the foundation that makes scale possible.

The DataHanle Perspective

The gap between enterprise data infrastructure and AI capability is not technical—it is semantic.

  • Observe: Understand how knowledge actually flows through your organization, where definitions diverge, and where context gets lost.
  • Understand: Build explicit semantic models that reflect business reality and can be shared between humans and AI agents.
  • Decide: Deploy AI orchestration with confidence, knowing that every decision is grounded in governed, shared context.
